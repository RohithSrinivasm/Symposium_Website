About Us

Saankethika 2020

The objective of the symposium is to bring the young budding engineering graduates together to present their findings and discuss future trends, directions and new challenges in the Electronics & Communication field. This symposium aims to intensify the information exchange of their ideas in theoretical research and practical developments.

This symposium can be used to discuss what and how the student community can facilitate the process of technology development. This would prove to be a boon to students. It will be a forum for new ideas and concepts and also would endeavour to examine the different dimensions of Electronics & Communication field.

Topic:

Paper Presentation

Description:

“Let your mind write the paper not your hand”

Students have a great platform to express their innovative ideas that could lead to great creations in technology. Take up the challenge to evoke your genuine ideas. Who knows this could bring you fame and glory!

Rules:

• Teams will have to bring hard copies of the full paper on the day of presentation.
• 2-3 members per team.
• Each team would be given a time span of 10 minutes to explain their ideas.
• Last date to submit : 26/01/2020.
• The judges decisions shall be binding and final.
• Papers must be submitted to saankethika2020@gmail.com
• Selected papers will be intimated through mail.

Topic:

Circuit Soldering

Description:

“Design circuits from right ones”

To all those tech enthusiasts out there, here is the chance to showcase your technical knowledge. Brainstorming, even the smallest tech facts may fetch you whooping advantages.

Rules:

Teams will be shortlisted based on the paper pen test. Shortlisted teams will be given a circuit description that you have to solve and solder the components according to the circuit layout you inferred. You will be scored on how well and neat your circuit is soldered and implemented with points for improvisation as well!!
• 2-3 members per team. The event organizers will help the lone wolves find their pack!
• Soldering kit and the electronic components will be provided.
• Show your soldering skills and have fun.
• Judges' decision will be final.

Topic:

Coding Contest

Description:

“Think Twice Code Once”

The best game plan is the one that has the fewest characters or fewest lines of code. So, code your way to rewards. .

Rules:

• There will be 2 rounds.
• 2 members per team.
• Preliminary Round: MCQ based.
• Final Round: Coding test.
• Programming languages allowed: C, C++, Java, Python.
• Internet access is not allowed.

Topic:

Technical and Non-Technical quiz

Description:

“A quiz is a form of game or mind sport.”

Wisdom begins with wonder. Even though you can compete as a team, a whole game can change based on the random knowledge of a person. RMD provides a platform to test your knowledge and activate your brain with some puzzling and brain storming questions. This is a team event to analyze technical skills, intelligence and presence of mind.

Rules:

• General MCQ's will be given.
• Pen and paper based event. Stationary will be provided.

Strictly 2 members per team

Topic:

Pictionary

Description:

“Coming together is BEGINNING;
Keeping together is PROGRESS;
Working together is SUCCESS!”

Pictionary is not an imitation but a creation. Here comes your game ‘Word Warcraft’ where picture speaks louder than words. Now here is the chance to show off your creativity, bondness and your individuality.

Rules:

• Use your creativity to picturize the given word
• Strictly 2 members per team.
• It is a face-off challenge where you compete with the other.
• Fast answers push your opponent to spot elimination and your chances of winning brightens.
• Alphabetical and other violating pictures are strictly prohibited.
